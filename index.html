To: Princess
My dearest Princess,
You are the softest hello in a noisy world and the sharpest eye-roll when I act foolish,
the way you laugh like sunlight spilling across an Aegean morning makes every ordinary day feel like a holiday,
I love the way your hair catches the light and the way your stubborn little grin announces you won the argument before it even started,
you are fierce in the best way — a tiny Greek hurricane with a heart made of honey and ancient sea-salt,
sometimes you are playful and a little mean in the sweetest way, and honestly I love you more for it,
you tease me like a practiced poet and correct me like a gentle teacher, and both make me want to be better,
your kindness is quiet but vast, like a harbor that always welcomes me home no matter how lost I feel,
you worry too much about small things and laugh too loudly at the big ones, and both of those things are infuriatingly adorable,
I love your hands — the ones that hold my phone when I'm distracted, that make soup when I'm tired, that tug me back when I wander,
you are sweetness wrapped in sharpness: baklava and lemon peel, syrup and spice, calm sea and quick storm,
I love that you are proud of where you come from, that your stories carry islands and olive trees and stubborn family love,
and I love teasing you about how fiercely you protect your secrets — you know you hide smiles like treasures and roll your eyes like punctuation,
sometimes I call you my little tyrant of charm and you frown, and that frown becomes the thing I memorize like a blessing,
when you speak in Greek words that I do not fully understand, I still hear home — a language that wraps me up and makes me braver,
you make ordinary plans into myths I want to live inside forever, and even your smallest wishes feel like instructions for how to be better,
thank you for being stubborn enough to argue with me and kind enough to forgive me when I lose,
thank you for being my soft place to land and my loud reason to laugh, for being myth and mischief and so much mercy,
I promise to love you through the silly, the sharp, the dramatic, and the quiet, to learn your rhythms and keep catching you when you jump,
because you are worth every brave thing I might do and every foolish arrow I might follow,
so stay stubborn, stay sweet, stay gloriously, maddeningly, beautifully Greek — and stay mine, Princess,
With all my ridiculous heart,
— Elrique
